# Paper Standard

Every formal publication or technical report produced by Oja AI Research must adhere to a strict structural standard to ensure clarity, rigor, and peer-review readiness.

## Definition of Done (DoD)
A research paper is only considered **DONE** and ready for publication (e.g., to arXiv or conference submission) when it contains the following mandatory sections:

- [ ] **Motivation:** Clear articulation of why this problem matters for African commerce (e.g., why Western models fail).
- [ ] **Methodology:** Detailed explanation of the architectural or data-curation choices made.
- [ ] **Dataset:** Transparent description of the data sources, languages covered, and anonymization pipelines.
- [ ] **Evaluation:** Rigorous benchmarking results (must include ACIB scores).
- [ ] **Limitations:** Honest disclosure of where the model or benchmark fails (e.g., poor performance in a specific dialect).
- [ ] **Ethics:** Discussion of safety, bias mitigation, and the economic impact on informal vendors.
- [ ] **Licensing:** Clear declaration of the license (e.g., CC-BY-4.0 for ACIB).
- [ ] **Reproducibility:** A dedicated section outlining exactly how a third-party researcher can reproduce the findings.
