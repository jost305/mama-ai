# The Oja AI Research Manifesto

## Why We Exist
For too long, artificial intelligence has been built on the assumptions of formal, Western economies. Global foundation models assume commerce happens in structured supermarkets with fixed prices, static inventory, and formal English. 

In Africa, commerce is a dynamic, multi-lingual, and deeply relational negotiation. It happens in open markets, on WhatsApp, and across fragmented supply chains.

**Oja AI Research** exists to build the intelligence layer for African commerce. We believe that to truly serve the African consumer and vendor, AI must be engineered from the ground up to understand the cultural, logistical, and linguistic realities of the continent.

---

## Our Core Principles

### 1. Intelligence Must Reflect Reality
Commerce intelligence should reflect African markets, languages, and consumer realities. A model that cannot distinguish between 'Oloyin' and 'Drum' beans, or understand the seasonal impact of the rainy season on tomato prices, is fundamentally broken for our users.

### 2. Reason over Memorization
Models should reason with live market information rather than memorize changing prices. Prices fluctuate daily. Our models (OjaLM) are taught the *logic* of commerce—how to negotiate, how to calculate bulk discounts, and how to assess trade-offs—while relying on external tools (MamaPrice) to retrieve real-time data.

### 3. Benchmarks Must Be Pristine
Benchmarks should be transparent, reproducible, and never used as training data. The African Commerce Intelligence Benchmark (ACIB) is our North Star. It is structurally decoupled from our training corpus (OjaData) to ensure our evaluations are scientifically rigorous and immune to contamination.

### 4. Build for the Ecosystem
Research artifacts should be documented and versioned so others can build on them. Oja AI Research does not just build closed products; we build infrastructure. By versioning ACIB, OjaData, and OjaLM independently, we empower the broader African NLP community to evaluate and improve their own models.

### 5. Open Collaboration
Open collaboration is encouraged where licensing and data governance permit. We release our benchmarks, taxonomies, and research papers publicly to accelerate the development of localized AI across the continent, while strictly protecting consumer privacy and vendor data.
