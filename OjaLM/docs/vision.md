# OjaLM

**OjaLM** is a family of foundation models built specifically for commerce across African markets.

Our mission is to build AI that understands how commerce actually works—from informal street markets to modern retail, from multilingual customer conversations to product intelligence, price reasoning, negotiation, and transactional workflows.

OjaLM is not designed to be another general-purpose chatbot.

It is designed to become the intelligence layer powering commerce across Africa.

MarketMama AI is the flagship application built on top of OjaLM.

---

# Mission

Our mission is to build the world's most capable foundation models for African commerce.

These models should understand:

* Products
* Markets
* Vendors
* Buyers
* Shopping behavior
* Negotiation
* Product discovery
* Regional commerce
* African languages
* Retail operations
* Transactional reasoning

Rather than replacing existing commerce systems, OjaLM aims to make them significantly more intelligent.

# Long-Term Vision

We envision OjaLM becoming the foundation of an open commerce intelligence ecosystem.

That ecosystem includes:

* **OjaLM** — Foundation models
* **OjaGraph** — Commerce knowledge graph
* **OjaData** — Commerce datasets
* **OjaBench** — Commerce evaluation benchmarks
* **Oja Studio** — Training and evaluation platform
* **Oja API** — Developer platform
* **MarketMama AI** — Consumer application

Together, these components create an AI infrastructure that enables developers, businesses, researchers, and consumers to build commerce-first intelligent systems.

# Philosophy

## Data over Parameters

Model size alone does not create useful intelligence.

High-quality, domain-specific data creates competitive advantage.

OjaLM will prioritize curated commerce knowledge over unnecessary parameter growth.

---

## Reasoning over Memorization

Prices, inventories, and product availability change constantly.

These should not be memorized inside model weights.

Instead:

* Live prices belong in MarketMama's data platform.
* Vendor inventory belongs in operational databases.
* Relationships belong in OjaGraph.

The model's responsibility is to reason over this information accurately.

---

## African-First, Globally Useful

OjaLM is designed around the realities of African commerce while remaining useful for developers worldwide.

Our objective is not merely multilingual translation but genuine understanding of local commerce, products, negotiation styles, and market behavior.

---

## Open Research

Whenever practical, OjaLM research will be transparent.

We believe reproducible research, benchmarks, technical reports, and open evaluation strengthen the AI ecosystem and accelerate innovation.

# Research Principles

OjaLM research follows these principles:

## Benchmark-Driven Development

Every model release must demonstrate measurable improvement using standardized evaluations.

Model quality will be validated through OjaBench.

---

## Reproducibility

Training procedures, datasets (where licensing permits), evaluation methods, and research documentation should be reproducible.

---

## Modular Architecture

Every major component should evolve independently.

This includes:

* Base models
* Knowledge graph
* Dataset pipeline
* Evaluation framework
* Training pipeline
* Applications

No single component should become a bottleneck for future innovation.

---

## Responsible AI

OjaLM should:

* minimize hallucinations
* provide transparent reasoning
* acknowledge uncertainty
* respect user privacy
* support responsible deployment

---

# Non-Goals

OjaLM is **not** intended to:

* Memorize live product prices
* Replace operational databases
* Replace knowledge graphs
* Store vendor inventory inside model weights
* Become a generic conversational chatbot
* Compete as a general-purpose language model

Instead, OjaLM will specialize in commerce intelligence.
